------MENU CARD------

Starters:

Vegetarian Starters:

Spring Rolls – Crispy rolls filled with vegetables, sometimes with noodles, served with dipping sauce.

Bruschetta – Toasted bread topped with tomatoes, basil, garlic, and olive oil.

Mozzarella Sticks – Deep-fried cheese sticks with a crispy coating, served with marinara sauce.

Non-Vegetarian Starters:

Chicken Wings – Spicy, tangy, or sweet chicken wings, often served with a dip.

Chicken Satay – Skewered, grilled chicken served with peanut sauce.

Fish Fingers – Crispy, breaded fish strips served with tartar sauce.

Soups & Light Starters:

Tomato Soup – A rich, creamy soup made from tomatoes and herbs.

Miso Soup – A Japanese soup with miso paste, tofu, and seaweed.

French Onion Soup – A deep-flavored soup with caramelized onions and melted cheese on top.

Biryani's:

Vegetarian Biryani's:

Paneer Biryani - A rich and creamy biryani where soft paneer cubes are marinated with yogurt and spices, then layered with rice for a delicious taste.

Mushroom Biryani - A mildly spiced biryani made with mushrooms, yogurt, and whole spices, giving it a unique earthy aroma.

Soya Chunk Biryani - A protein-rich vegetarian biryani made with soya chunks, which absorb the flavors of the spices and mimic the texture of meat.

Non-Vegetarian Biryani's:

Chicken Biryani - The most popular biryani, made with marinated chicken, basmati rice, and a blend of aromatic spices, slow-cooked to perfection.

Mutton Biryani - Prepared with tender, slow-cooked mutton (goat meat) and aromatic basmati rice, making it rich and flavorful. Popular in Hyderabad, Kolkata, and Tamil Nadu.

Keema Biryani - Minced mutton or chicken is used instead of whole meat pieces, making it juicy and flavorful.

Mocktails:

Fresh Lime Soda - A refreshing drink made with lemon juice, soda, sugar, or salt—served chilled for a tangy and fizzy taste.

Virgin Mojito - A non-alcoholic version of the classic mojito, made with mint, lime, sugar, and soda, giving it a refreshing flavor.
